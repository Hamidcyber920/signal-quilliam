FAQs

SIGNAL @ The Quilliam is an annual developer conference held inside the Abdullah Quilliam Society — Britain's first, established in 1887. It brings together developers, business leaders and innovators for two days of keynotes, workshops and hands-on learning, in a Grade II listed Victorian terrace.
SIGNAL @ The Quilliam takes place September 14–15, 2026 at the Abdullah Quilliam Society, 1-10 Brougham Terrace, Liverpool L6 1AE, United Kingdom.
We are planning a fully in-person experience. Select keynote content will be available on-demand the week after the event concludes. Enterprise pass holders receive early access to all recordings.
Yes — sponsorship packages are available. Contact signal@quilliam.org for the sponsorship prospectus. All sponsorship contributions also support the Abdullah Quilliam Society heritage fund.
Register using the form on this page. Select your pass type, complete the booking form and you'll receive a confirmation email with your NFC badge QR code within 24 hours.
Community Pass: £149 · Builder Pass: £349 · Enterprise Pass: £699. Team discount of 15% applies automatically for 3+ Enterprise passes. Student and bursary places available — contact signal@quilliam.org.
Full refunds are available for cancellations made by 14 August 2026. Cancellations after this date are subject to a £30 processing fee. Contact signal@quilliam.org to cancel.
We accept Visa, Mastercard, Amex, and bank transfer for group bookings. All transactions are processed securely via Stripe.
Absolutely. All food served is halal. The Abdullah Quilliam Society has designated prayer spaces available throughout the day. Prayer times will be announced from the main stage. All dietary requirements — vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, dairy-free — are catered for.
Builder and Enterprise pass holders receive an NFC-enabled badge pre-loaded with their attendee profile and Twilio API credentials. You can reprogramme it at the DevNet desk to trigger webhooks, send messages, or unlock swag. Your badge is literally an API endpoint.
